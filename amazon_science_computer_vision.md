## 1. What Amazon Science Is

[Amazon Science](https://www.amazon.science/) is Amazon's public-facing portal for its scientific research community. It covers Amazon's work in AI, machine learning, computer vision, robotics, economics, quantum technologies, and sustainability, and showcases publications, blog posts, conference activity, academic collaborations, and open science/career roles.

Amazon frames its science mission around having "an impact at scale" — using research to power customer-facing products (Alexa, Amazon.com search/ads, Rekognition, Amazon Go, robotics fleets) rather than purely academic output.

### Site structure
- **Research** → Research areas, Publications, Conferences
- **News & blog** → Amazon Science Blog, News
- **Collaborations** → Amazon Research Awards, Amazon Nova AI Challenge, university research hubs (MIT, CMU, Johns Hopkins, Columbia, Max Planck Society, UCLA, UIUC, USC, UT Austin, Virginia Tech, University of Washington, Howard, Hampton, IIT Bombay, Tennessee State)
- **Resources** → Code and datasets, AGI Labs, Amazon Nova
- **Careers** → Open roles, Amazon Scholars, Postdoctoral Science Program

## 3. Careers in Computer Vision at Amazon Science

URL: https://www.amazon.science/careers

### Career program overview
Amazon Science's careers section funnels into three main tracks:
1. **Open roles** (via Amazon.jobs, filtered to science/applied-science categories)
2. **Amazon Scholars** — part-time faculty roles for academics who want to work on industry-scale problems while continuing to teach/research at their home universities
3. **Postdoctoral Science Program** — early-career researchers work alongside experienced industry scientists post-PhD

Amazon's pitch: *"Our scientists' ability to have an impact at scale allows us to attract some of the brightest minds across diverse fields including artificial intelligence, robotics, computer vision, economics, and sustainability."*

### Typical computer vision roles posted
Roles recur across many Amazon orgs (not a single "CV team"): Amazon Industrial Robotics, Amazon International Machine Learning, AWS, Alexa AI, Amazon Ads, Amazon Shopping/Detail Page, Amazon Devices, Amazon Logistics. Examples seen in current postings:

- **Senior Applied Scientist – Computer Vision (International Machine Learning)** — generative models (image/video/3D) to enhance shopping/catalog experience. Requires PhD, 4+ years applied ML, 5+ top-tier publications (CVPR/ICCV/ECCV/NeurIPS).
- **Sr. Applied Scientist, Robot Perception (Amazon Industrial Robotics)** — object detection, segmentation, tracking, depth estimation, scene understanding, sensor fusion, 3D perception; expects publication at CVPR/ICCV/ECCV/ICRA/NeurIPS.
- **Applied Science Intern / PhD internships – Computer Vision** — work on Vision-Language Models, object recognition/detection, facial recognition, image retrieval, video understanding, and robotics; tied to products like **Amazon Rekognition, Amazon Go, Visual Search**. 2026 internship pay range cited: **$136,000–$223,400/year** (US).
- General applied-science roles across Ads, Search, Alexa, Logistics, and Robotics frequently list computer vision as one required/intersecting skill alongside NLP, LLMs/GenAI, reinforcement learning, and robotics.

### Common qualifications sought
- PhD (or equivalent experience) in CS, ML, robotics, statistics, or related quantitative field
- Strong publication record at top CV/ML venues: **CVPR, ICCV, ECCV, NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, ACL, KDD, AISTATS**
- Programming in **Python**, Java, or C++
- Experience with deep learning, large-scale ML systems, and increasingly **vision-language models / multimodal / generative AI**
- For robotics-adjacent CV roles: sensor fusion, 3D perception, simulation experience

### Internal infrastructure mentioned for science roles
- Custom AI silicon: **Trainium and Inferentia** chips
- **Project Rainier** AI supercomputer
- Internal **Amazon Machine Learning Conference** for showcasing science work
- Patents and publications are explicitly encouraged/incentivized as part of career growth

### Other career-adjacent programs
- **Amazon Research Awards** — unrestricted funding + AWS credits for academic/non-profit researchers
- **Amazon Nova AI Challenge** — competitive program for student teams
- **AI PhD Fellowship Program** ($68M, 100+ students, 9 universities) — funds doctoral research in ML, computer vision, and NLP
- **University research hubs/collaborations** spanning MIT, CMU, Johns Hopkins, Columbia, Max Planck Society, UCLA, UIUC, USC, UT Austin, Virginia Tech, University of Washington, Howard, Hampton, IIT Bombay, Tennessee State University

## 4. Key Takeaways

1. **Computer vision is treated as one of ~12 official "research areas"** at Amazon Science, alongside automated reasoning, cloud/systems, conversational AI, economics, information/knowledge management, machine learning, operations research, quantum tech, robotics, search/IR, security/privacy, and sustainability.
2. **Current CV research emphasis (2025–2026)** is shifting heavily toward **vision-language models, generative diffusion models (Nova Canvas/Reel), parameter-efficient fine-tuning, and trust/safety (deepfake detection)** — alongside classic applied CV (segmentation, defect detection, robot perception).
3. **CV careers at Amazon are distributed, not centralized** — opportunities sit inside Robotics, Devices, Ads, AWS, Alexa, and Retail/Shopping teams rather than one "Computer Vision department."
4. Amazon explicitly courts **academic talent** through Scholars, Postdoc, Research Awards, the AI PhD Fellowship, and numerous named university hubs — many of which intersect with CV research.
5. Compensation data point: 2026 PhD CV internship range in the US was listed at **$136,000–$223,400/year**, suggestive of how competitively Amazon prices applied-science talent in this space.
